My Approach

Why now

Leading a small business has been the goal since day one of my career. Nine years in, with an MBA from Wisconsin behind me, it's time to go do it.

What I bring

At Intuit, I led the launch of a new QuickBooks product from zero to $60M in annual revenue within two years, rallying a cross-functional team of about fifteen people across engineering, design, analytics, marketing, and customer success around a clear vision. Before that, I worked in consulting at Deloitte. I know small business accounting, chemical manufacturing, and — from my first job — house painting.

What that experience actually transfers: getting a cross-functional group aligned on a goal, disciplined project management, and a habit of checking the data before making a call. That's the job of running a small company.

What I'm looking for

A company generating $1M–$7M in revenue, based in the Twin Cities area, western Wisconsin, or along the I-94 corridor toward Eau Claire, Madison, and Milwaukee.

How I think about it

I'm buying one company to run for the long term, not to resell. If you've spent years building something that takes care of your customers and your people, I want to keep that intact — not restructure it into something unrecognizable.

If that sounds like your business, let's talk.
